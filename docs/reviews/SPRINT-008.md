# SPRINT 008 - Narrative Reduction & Section Ownership

## Objective

Reduce repetition across the homepage and primary website pages while assigning one clear narrative job to every section.

## Sections Changed

- Hero
- Capability Journey
- Missing Layer
- A New Kind of Organization
- Imagine
- Platform / OIS section
- Organization Mirror
- Company
- Footer closing
- Platform page
- Thinking page
- Partners page
- Company page
- Contact page
- Capability card CTA labels
- Brand Canon documentation naming references

## Statements Removed

- Direct repetition of "organizations understand themselves" outside the Hero.
- Direct "AI alone is not enough" phrasing from Company language.
- Full knowledge-to-self-enabling hierarchy from the Missing Layer section.
- Redundant Hero-like claims from Thinking and Company.
- Over-explanatory Hero support text.

## Statements Consolidated

- The capability progression is now owned by the Capability Journey.
- The Missing Layer now owns only the shift from fragmented knowledge to shared meaning.
- Thinking now owns authority and inevitability.
- Company now owns conviction.
- Footer now owns the move from interest to conversation.
- Existing product card links now state the relevant product destination.

## Brand Canon Language

Updated internal naming references without renaming asset files:

- BC-001: Organizational Understanding
- BC-002: Capability Journey
- BC-003: Transparent Intelligence
- BC-004: Organizational Intelligence Elements

## Remaining Risks

- The site still contains many legacy routes outside the new premium architecture.
- Platform and Capability Journey still share BC-002 and need clearer visual governance.
- Mobile typography and scrolling fatigue remain outside this sprint.
- Product routes still risk pulling the website back toward a product-portfolio feel.

## Recommendations for Sprint 009

Make Sprint 009 a Brand Canon Governance sprint:

1. Define exact rules for BC-001 through BC-004.
2. Clarify which page owns which Brand Canon asset.
3. Decide how product cards may use cropped Canon details.
4. Create forbidden-use examples.
5. Preserve the reduced narrative before adding new visuals.
