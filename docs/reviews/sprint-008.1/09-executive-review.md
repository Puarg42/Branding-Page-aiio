# 09 - Executive Review

## Purpose

Review the homepage from six executive perspectives before completing Sprint 008.1.

## CEO Perspective

| Question | Finding |
| --- | --- |
| What is immediately clear? | The page opens with a strong category claim: future-ready organizations need a new form of self-understanding. |
| What remains unclear? | The direct business value becomes clear later than the category ambition. A CEO may ask earlier: what changes operationally for my company? |
| Strongest headline | "The future belongs to organizations that understand themselves." |
| Weakest headline | "The system behind the journey." It is accurate, but less quotable than the surrounding statements. |
| Where does momentum drop? | After the "Imagine..." sequence, the emotional pacing is strong, but the transition into CEO Moment risks feeling like a second imagination section. |
| Where does trust increase? | Trust increases in "Why aiio exists" because the tone becomes calm, mission-led and non-promotional. |

## Cross-Perspective Findings

| Review Area | Finding |
| --- | --- |
| Immediately clear | aiio is positioning Organizational Intelligence as a new category, not a product portfolio. |
| Still unclear | The homepage could make the operational/business consequence clearer without becoming product-heavy. |
| Strongest headline overall | "The future belongs to organizations that understand themselves." |
| Weakest headline overall | "The system behind the journey." |
| Narrative momentum loss | The emotional middle contains adjacent ideas that are individually strong but close in function: Imagine, CEO Moment and Organization Mirror. |
| Trust increase | Trust increases whenever the page becomes calm, precise and infrastructure-oriented rather than promotional. |

## Editorial Implications

- Keep the Hero headline. It is quotable and category-defining.
- Reconsider "The system behind the journey" in a later sprint. It is accurate, but not yet memorable.
- Preserve the Why Now section as the investor-grade argument.
- Watch the emotional middle for repetition. It should feel cinematic, not extended.
- Strengthen the bridge from category vision to business consequence without adding product explanation.
